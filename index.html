Built For Plumbing Contractors

It was never about getting leads.
It's about knowing the job before you go to site.

Hermia is built specifically for plumbing contractors — businesses across Australia, Canada, New Zealand, the UK, Ireland and the United States running Simpro or AroFlo, drowning in high volumes of enquiries that all need qualifying before anyone can act.

Every enquiry is run through your question set — what's wrong, where it is, how bad it is, and since when — with photos and video to back it up. Vague answers get pushed back on until they're specific enough to act on, so tire kickers filter themselves out before they ever reach you.

By the time you see the job, you already have the full picture — not a guess you make after rolling a truck. And it's not just sitting in an inbox: it's already booked in your CRM, against a real technician's real availability, before your office has even picked up the phone.

And Hermia becomes an additional lead channel of its own. Once a lead has been through qualification, Hermia remembers them permanently — so the next time that same customer needs help, they don't message a competitor or run a Google search. They message Hermia, go through the same fast qualification, and the job lands back in your CRM. The lead stays completely yours.

How It Works

Not a chatbot. A full intake system.

Here's exactly what happens — and exactly how — between an enquiry landing and a booked job appearing on your calendar.

01 — Intake

Every enquiry read the moment it lands.

Gmail and Outlook are monitored around the clock. Direct emails, forwarded messages from property managers and agents, leads buried inside platform emails — Hermia reads each one, works out whether it's a genuine plumbing enquiry, and finds the actual person who needs help, even when the email came from a business on their behalf.

The filter is trade-specific — it's trained to recognise what a genuine plumbing enquiry actually looks like, not just scan for keywords. Invoices, alerts, marketing and newsletters are filtered out; so are enquiries that are clearly for a different trade entirely. Real leads never slip through. Attachments are opened and read, not just noted — a PDF work order from a property manager, a photo of a leaking pipe, a scanned job sheet: Hermia extracts the address, the issue, and any reference numbers straight out of the file, so nothing sits unread in an inbox waiting for someone to open it.

The lead receives a clean, branded email within moments — introduced as your digital assistant, under your name — with a unique Customer ID and one-tap buttons to continue on WhatsApp, pre-sorted by urgency.

02 — Qualification

Your trade's questions. Genuinely answered.

On WhatsApp — Hermia's own Meta-verified business number — the lead goes through a step-by-step qualification built for plumbing work: what's leaking, blocked or not working, where it is, whether water is actively running, whether it's been shut off at the mains or the fixture, since when, and what's already been tried.

If an answer is too vague to act on, Hermia doesn't shrug and move on. "The tap's broken" gets a reply naming exactly what's missing — and when the lead answers, it's merged with what they already said, so nobody repeats themselves. Nonsense and spam are filtered without a human ever seeing them. That same filter is what removes tire kickers: the unserious drop off at the first real question; the serious finish in minutes.

Active flooding, a gas smell, or no hot water at all escalates instantly. Urgency keywords cut through every queue.

Handled in the conversation

  • Voice notes — transcribed and treated like typed answers
  • Photo & video bursts — ten photos and a clip? Collected as one batch, stored permanently, delivered as a single branded gallery link on the job. What's said in videos is transcribed into the notes

  • Pre-approved templates only — every message Hermia sends is on-script; nothing is freeform AI

Then it books the closest available match.

The job is created, the technician is assigned, and the appointment lands on your CRM calendar as a real scheduled activity — exactly as if your best office admin had checked the whiteboard and pencilled it in. Your customer gets a confirmed time and the technician's name before the conversation ends. Your tech gets a booked job. Nobody plays phone tag. Simply put: with Simpro, we book the lead for you.

How It Works

How Hermia qualifies your plumbing leads.

Most "AI chatbots" just answer questions. Hermia does something different: it runs every enquiry through a structured qualification process before it ever reaches your phone, so what lands in your inbox is a real, bookable job — not a half-finished conversation.

The moment someone messages you on WhatsApp about a plumbing issue, Hermia takes over the back-and-forth and works through a purpose-built set of questions designed specifically for plumbing jobs — not a generic template borrowed from another trade. Here's what it actually asks, and why each question matters.

The Problem Itself

What's actually wrong — a leak, a blockage, no hot water, a burst pipe. Hermia reads the reply in plain English, however messy or informal, and turns it into a clear, professional job description your team can act on immediately.

What's Affected

Which fixture or system needs attention — hot water unit, toilet, tap, drain, pipe, or sewer line. If the customer mentions a brand or model, Hermia captures that too, so you know what you're walking into before you arrive.

The Site Address

Captured, verified against your actual service area, and confirmed back with the customer before anything proceeds. No wasted trips to the wrong suburb or an address that doesn't check out.

Preferred Timing

When the customer wants the job done, resolved into an exact day and time — not a vague "sometime this week."

Site Access

Whether someone will be home, whether the property is locked or restricted, and whether your plumber will need a key, code, or site induction to get in.

Hazards

Anything your plumber should know before attending — pets, restricted areas, confined spaces, mould, or structural damage. If there's nothing to report, Hermia confirms that too, so your technician isn't left guessing.

Water or Gas Isolation

Whether the supply is already off, or whether your plumber will need to shut it off on arrival, and where the shut-off point is if the customer knows.

Residential or Commercial

Because the two often mean different pricing, different access requirements, and different jobs entirely.

Billing Contact

Who's responsible for payment — the customer themselves, a property manager, an owners corporation, or a managing agent.

A Price Estimate

Based on your own pricing structure, Hermia gives the customer a clear estimate before booking and confirms they're happy to proceed. If they push back on price, Hermia hands them straight to your contact details rather than guessing at a negotiation.

Why this matters for you

Every one of these questions exists for one reason: so that by the time a job lands in front of you, you already know what the problem is, where it is, who's paying, whether you can get in, what to bring, and what it's roughly going to cost. No back-and-forth. No arriving to find out the water was never turned off, or the customer wasn't told about the callout fee, or the address was never confirmed.

If a customer's answer is unclear, vague, or doesn't actually answer what was asked, Hermia doesn't guess. It asks again, in plain language, until it has something your team can genuinely act on. Nothing gets passed through half-finished.

It's not a chatbot

Hermia isn't a script that repeats itself or a generic FAQ bot bolted onto WhatsApp. It's a purpose-built qualification system, tuned specifically for how plumbing jobs actually work, built to filter out spam and time-wasters, and designed to hand your team a complete, ready-to-book job every time.

Safety & Site Access

Isolations, hazards and how to get in.

Isolations is its own safety question, asked directly to the lead — it captures whether the water has been isolated at the mains or at the fixture before a technician can safely start work, and whether any gas or electrical isolation is also needed for the job. That's the difference between a plumber knowing the mains are already shut off versus discovering an active leak on arrival. It's stored as its own field, separate from general notes, so it shows as a distinct checklist item — never buried in a paragraph.

Hazards covers anything else on site — a dog on the property, restricted areas, awkward access under a house or in a roof cavity — kept distinct from isolations rather than folded into general safety notes. Access Notes covers gate codes, locked rooms, and who to call on arrival, so a technician is never standing at a locked gate with no way in.

Safety fields

Behind The Scenes

How Hermia keeps your CRM clean, every time.

Before Hermia creates anything, it checks what you already have — matching every lead against your existing customers and sites by phone and email, so nothing is ever duplicated. Every lead falls into one of three tiers.

Tier 1 — Match Found

The lead matches an existing customer at a site you already have on file. Hermia doesn't touch the customer or site record at all — it simply adds the new job with everything the lead just told it.

Tier 2 — New Site

The customer's on file, but this is a new address. Hermia leaves the customer record exactly as it is and creates only the new site — called "Creating Your Site" in AroFlo, or "Creating New Location" in Simpro — so your existing customer history is never touched.

Tier 3 — Brand New

No match at all. Hermia creates a brand-new customer record and a brand-new site together, fully populated from the qualification — so the very first job for a new customer is ready to go, start to finish.

In Simpro, every answer from the qualification writes straight into your CRM's own fields. In AroFlo, the client and location details are filled in automatically, and the full qualification — every answer, every detail — is delivered to you as a WhatsApp message instead.
