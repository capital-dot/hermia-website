Built For Fire & ESM Contractors

It was never about getting leads.
It's about knowing the job before you go to site.

Hermia is built specifically for Fire & ESM (Essential Safety Measures) commercial contractors — Australian businesses running Simpro or AroFlo, drowning in high volumes of enquiries that all need qualifying before anyone can act.

Every enquiry is put through your question set: what's wrong, where, how bad, since when — with photos and video to prove it. Vague answers get pushed back on. Tire kickers filter themselves out. You get the full picture before you go on a job — not after you've rolled a truck — and it lands in your CRM booked against a real technician's real availability, before your office has picked up the phone.

And Hermia becomes an additional lead channel of its own. Once a lead has been through qualification, Hermia remembers them permanently — so the next time that same customer needs help, they don't message a competitor or run a Google search. They message Hermia, go through the same fast qualification, and the job lands back in your CRM. The lead stays completely yours.

How It Works

Not a chatbot. A full intake system.

Here's exactly what happens — and exactly how — between an enquiry landing and a booked job appearing on your calendar.

01 — Intake

Every enquiry read the moment it lands.

Gmail and Outlook are monitored around the clock. Direct emails, forwarded messages from property managers and facilities teams, leads buried inside platform emails — Hermia reads each one, works out whether it's a genuine enquiry, and finds the actual person who needs help, even when the email came from a business on their behalf.

Invoices, alerts and newsletters are filtered out. Real leads never slip through. The lead receives a clean, branded email within moments — introduced as your digital assistant, under your name — with a unique Customer ID and one-tap buttons to continue on WhatsApp, pre-sorted by urgency.

What the lead sees
1Enquiry sent at 9:41pm — office closed
29:42pm: branded reply arrives from "your office" with their Customer ID
3They tap one button: needs attention today / within 2–3 days / planning ahead
4WhatsApp opens, already knowing who they are — qualification begins
02 — Qualification

Your trade's questions. Genuinely answered.

On WhatsApp — Hermia's own Meta-verified business number — the lead goes through a step-by-step qualification built for Fire & ESM work: which system or asset is affected, what's happening, isolation and safety requirements, site access and key requirements.

If an answer is too vague to act on, Hermia doesn't shrug and move on. "The panel's playing up" gets a reply naming exactly what's missing — and when the lead answers, it's merged with what they already said, so nobody repeats themselves. Nonsense and spam are filtered without a human ever seeing them. That same filter is what removes tire kickers: the unserious drop off at the first real question; the serious finish in minutes.

A gas smell or active hazard escalates instantly. Urgency keywords cut through every queue.

Handled in the conversation
  • Messy typing, slang & Australian shorthand — understood, cleaned, and written up professionally
  • Voice notes — transcribed and treated like typed answers
  • Photo & video bursts — ten photos and a clip? Collected as one batch, stored permanently, delivered as a single branded gallery link on the job. What's said in videos is transcribed into the notes
  • Follow-ups merged — partial answers are remembered; the lead only supplies what's missing
  • Pre-approved templates only — every message Hermia sends is on-script; nothing is freeform AI
03 — Real Scheduling

Booked against your actual calendar — not "we'll call you back."

This is where Hermia goes further than any intake bot. When the lead names their availability — "Tuesday morning" — Hermia pulls your actual staff list and your actual schedule from your CRM, scans the next 20 days, and works out which of your technicians is genuinely free at the time the customer asked for. Then it books the closest available match.

The job is created, the technician is assigned, and the appointment lands on your CRM calendar as a real scheduled activity — exactly as if your best office admin had checked the whiteboard and pencilled it in. Your customer gets a confirmed time and the technician's name before the conversation ends. Your tech gets a booked job. Nobody plays phone tag.

04 — Pricing

A real estimate — from your rates, not a guess.

During onboarding, you set your pricing criteria — what a defect rectification, an inspection, or a callout roughly costs at your rates. When a qualified lead describes their situation, Hermia matches it against your tiers and gives them a real figure before the job is booked. The "how much roughly?" conversation happens without touching your office — and price shoppers reveal themselves before costing you a cent of admin.

If they accept: the agreed figure is logged on the job record — no confusion when the job hits your schedule. If they negotiate: they're handed straight to the negotiation contact you chose at onboarding, and you're told they're reaching out about pricing before they contact you. The job lands in your CRM either way.

An additional lead channel, built on trust.

Every lead keeps their Customer ID forever. Once they've been through qualification once, Hermia remembers exactly who they are, which of your customers they belong to, and what they needed last time. No re-qualifying, no re-typing, no duplicate record. "Hi again — another issue at the same site?"

That memory is what turns Hermia into an additional lead channel of its own. A facilities manager or past customer who trusted the experience once doesn't message a competitor or run a Google search next time — they message Hermia, go through the exact same qualification, and the new job lands straight back in your CRM against the same customer, fully briefed. Repeat business and referrals flow in with zero admin — and the lead stays completely yours: they talk to your assistant, under your name. Hermia stays invisible.

Inside Your CRM

Exactly what lands in Simpro — and how it gets there.

It starts with nothing more than an email and a photo. The moment a lead's first message and images come in, Hermia is already writing the record — fully synced to your live Simpro account, no CSV, no manual entry, no copy-paste. By the time you open the job, the Customer and the Lead are already built, in your naming conventions, in your custom fields.

Customer Module

Who they are, and how urgent it is.

First name and last name are captured from the very first message, so the customer is greeted properly and matched correctly against your existing records — phone and email are both stored as matching keys, so a returning customer is recognised even if they use a different email or number the second time.

Urgency is written as a custom field directly on the Customer — Emergency, Critical, or Standard — so you can triage at a glance without opening the job. Estimate Amount is also written to the Customer record: the figure the lead was quoted, and whether they agreed to it or want to discuss it further.

Customer fields
  • First Name / Last Name — identity & matching
  • Phone — matching key
  • Email — second matching key
  • Urgency — Emergency / Critical / Standard
  • Estimate Amount — quoted or agreed price
  • Billing Entity — who's accountable for the invoice
Lead & Job Module

What's wrong, where it is, and what's needed to fix it.

Contact phone and email travel with the job as well as the customer, so whoever's dispatching never has to jump back to the customer card. Asset Type tells the technician what they're dealing with before they leave the office — a panel, sprinklers, or hydrants — so the right tools and parts go on the truck.

Fault Code is a standardised code for the specific fault type, written to its own field so faults can be tracked and reported on across your whole customer base over time. Defect Status records where the issue currently sits — active, intermittent, or resolved pending confirmation — so nobody has to re-read the whole job history to know what's still outstanding.

Lead / Job fields
  • Contact Phone / Email — travels with the job
  • Asset Type — panel, sprinklers, or hydrants
  • Fault Code — standardised fault classification
  • Defect Status — active / intermittent / resolved-pending
  • Confirmation Date — customer's stated availability, drives booking
Safety & Site Access

Isolations, hazards and how to get in.

Isolations is its own safety question, asked directly to the lead — it captures whether any power, circuits or equipment need to be electrically isolated (switched off or disconnected) before a technician can safely start work. For a fire or electrical job, that's the difference between a tech knowing the main switchboard is already isolated versus discovering live equipment on arrival. It's stored as its own field, separate from general notes, so it shows as a distinct checklist item — never buried in a paragraph.

Hazards covers anything else on site — a dog on the property, asbestos, restricted areas — kept distinct from isolations rather than folded into general safety notes. Access Notes covers gate codes, locked rooms, and who to call on arrival, so a technician is never standing at a locked gate with no way in.

Safety fields
  • Isolations — power/circuits needing shutdown before work starts
  • Hazards — other on-site risks, flagged separately
  • Safety Notes — general site conditions
  • Access Notes — gate codes, keys, on-site contact
Site Address

Typed loosely. Stored Simpro-clean.

Whatever the lead types — "the place on Collins Street, Melbourne" — is parsed and written into Simpro exactly the way Simpro needs it: street address, suburb, state and postcode as their own fields, feeding a properly structured Site record rather than one messy line of text.

Photos and videos the lead sends are attached to the same job as a permanent gallery link, so a technician can see the fault before they've even left the office — not just read a description of it.

Site fields

  • State
  • Postcode
  • Photos & Videos — gallery link, synced to the job
