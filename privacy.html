1. Statutory Compliance & Scope

This Privacy Policy governs the processing of personal information by Hermia ("the Platform", "we", "us", or "our"). This policy is engineered to achieve strict statutory compliance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) (including the Australian Privacy Principles), the UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 45 of 2021 on Personal Data Protection (PDPL), and other global data protection standards applicable to real estate transactions.

2. Data Processor vs. Data Controller Separation

The Client (the subscriber, real estate agency, or independent agent) acts strictly as the Data Controller under applicable law. The Client retains sole legal ownership, authority, and ultimate liability over the collection methods, consent frameworks, and structural use of all prospect, buyer, and tenant information. Hermia acts exclusively as a Data Processor. Hermia executes technical data ingestion, sorting, pipeline routing, and formatting algorithms solely under the direct instruction of the Client and the configurations set within the software pipeline.

Zero-Credential Access Control

Hermia limits data liabilities by utilizing advanced API-driven connections and tokenized OAuth 2.0 authentication architecture to read and write database modifications to your Customer Relationship Management (CRM) tools and mail communication boxes. At no stage does Hermia store, cache, or maintain visible access to your raw cleartext account passwords.

3. Categorization of Processed Information

To facilitate zero-friction operations, the system filters data into three distinct processing domains:

  • Operational Metadata (Corporate): Standard commercial identity inputs including billing profiles, company registration metrics, ABN configurations, and authorized staff contact records required for maintenance.
  • Front-End Lead Payloads (Inbound): Lead data attributes (including names, electronic addresses, phone variables, and property query parameters) securely captured via automated webhooks from third-party advertising real estate channels.
  • Pipeline Transaction Logs (Outbound): Structured, system-generated response confirmations and structured scheduling criteria recorded via automated communications protocols (such as SMS networks or WhatsApp application endpoints).

4. Volatile Memory Execution & Cross-Border Routing

Hermia processes lead telemetry records within secure cloud routing memory blocks. Data retention is strictly locked to the window of execution required to structure, cross-verify, and instantly commit the record into the Client's active CRM system. Where legal requirements demand international routing between Australia and the UAE, transfer actions are protected via end-to-end 256-bit encryption layers. All operational sub-processors and network traffic firewalls used to secure the platform are contractually obligated to maintain rigorous enterprise compliance controls.
